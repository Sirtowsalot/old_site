Cascade Mountains

Where are the mountains?

The Cascade Range or Cascades is a major mountain range of North America. It's located in and between Oregon and Washington state.

What kind of plate boundary is it?

The Cascade Mountains have formed due to a convergent boundary, effectively making them a convergent boundary. (Cont. O-C)

Which plates are involved?

Here the Juan de Fuca Plate sinks beneath the North American Plate. This means that the North American Plate and the Juan de Fuca plate are involved.

What type of mountains are they?

The cascade mountains include both non volcanic mountains, like the north cascades, wile the high cascades are made up of volcanic mountains.

What type of volcanic collection is it?

The cascade mountains are a continental arc collection of volcanic mountains

Is Sea Floor Created or Destroyed?

At the cascade mountains sea floor is being destroyed. The oceanic crust is being pushed under the continental crust.

Why do Earthquakes Occur Here?

It occurs from the upper mantle scraping against the Continental Crust, and this produces the magma or lava that comes out of it.
